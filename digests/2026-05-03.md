### [Crab: A Semantics-Aware Checkpoint/Restore Runtime for Agent Sandboxes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28138v1)
**Authors:** Tianyuan Wu, Chaokun Chang, Lunxi Cao | **Published:** 2026-04-30

**Core Problem:** Existing checkpoint and restore approaches for autonomous agents are either incomplete or too expensive due to an agent-OS semantic gap.

**Approach:**
- Developed a semantics-aware checkpoint/restore runtime called Crab to bridge the agent-OS gap.
- Crab identifies and checkpoints only recovery-relevant state changes, leveraging sparsity in agent turns.
- Evaluation on various agent frameworks and sandbox environments.

**Results:**
- Crab demonstrates significant reductions in checkpointing overhead without compromising correctness.
- Outperforms application-level recovery and full per-turn checkpointing in terms of efficiency and effectiveness.
- Highlights the potential for optimizing checkpointing strategies based on agent-OS semantic awareness.

- Suggests future research directions for achieving more realistic and interactive visual generation.

### [Defending Quantum Classifiers against Adversarial Perturbations through Quantum Autoencoders](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28176v1)
**Authors:** Emma Andrews, Sahan Sanjaya, Prabhat Mishra | **Published:** 2026-04-30

**Approach:**

- Evaluated the framework on image classification tasks using variational quantum classifiers.

**Results:**
- Demonstrated the effectiveness of quantum autoencoders in defending against adversarial perturbations.
- Showed that the proposed framework can improve the robustness of quantum classifiers without requiring adversarial training.
- Highlights the potential of quantum autoencoders for enhancing the security of quantum machine learning models.
