## 📄 Papers

### [Rethinking XAI Evaluation: A Human-Centered Audit of Shapley Benchmarks in High-Stakes Settings](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22662v1)
**Authors:** Inês Oliveira e Silva, Sérgio Jesus, Iker Perez | **Published:** 2026-04-24

**Core Problem:** The alignment of quantitative proxies for explainable AI (XAI) with human utility is unverified, leading to potential misalignment in high-stakes settings.

**Approach:**
- Unified amortized framework to isolate semantic differences between Shapley variants.
- Evaluation across four risk datasets and a realistic fraud-detection environment.
- Involvement of professional analysts and 3,735 case reviews for empirical assessment.

**Results:**
- Fundamental misalignment between standard quantitative metrics and human utility.
- Sparsity and faithfulness metrics do not capture the full scope of explainability.
- The need for human-centered audits in XAI evaluation is highlighted.

### [LLM Safety From Within: Detecting Harmful Content with Internal Representations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18519)
**Authors:** Unknown | **Published:** 2026-04-27

**Core Problem:** State-of-the-art guard models for detecting harmful content rely solely on terminal-layer representations, overlooking safety-relevant features in internal layers.

**Approach:**
- Introduction of SIREN, a lightweight guard model harnessing internal features.
- Identification of safety neurons via linear probing and combination through an adaptive layer-weighted strategy.
- Comprehensive evaluation across multiple benchmarks.

**Results:**
- SIREN substantially outperforms state-of-the-art open-source guard models.
- Superior generalization to unseen scenarios and contexts.
- Significant reduction in required trainable parameters.

### [Agentic World Modeling: Foundations, Capabilities, Laws, and Beyond](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22748v1)
**Authors:** Meng Chu, Xuan Billy Zhang, Kevin Qinghong Lin | **Published:** 2026-04-24

**Core Problem:** The term "world model" carries different meanings across research communities, lacking a unified framework for understanding environment dynamics in AI systems.

**Approach:**
- Introduction of a "levels x laws" taxonomy for categorizing world models.
- Definition of three capability levels: L1 Predictor, L2 Simulator, and L3 Evolver.
- Identification of four governing laws for world models.

**Results:**
- A structured approach to understanding and developing world models for AI systems.
- Clarity on the capabilities and limitations of current world modeling approaches.
- Foundation for further research into advanced world modeling capabilities.

### [What People See (and Miss) About Generative AI Risks: Perceptions of Failures, Risks, and Who Should Address Them](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22654v1)
**Authors:** Megan Li, Wendy Bickersteth, Ningjing Tang | **Published:** 2026-04-24

**Core Problem:** Limited understanding of public perceptions of Generative AI (GenAI) risks and their associated failure modes.

**Approach:**
- Development of a survey instrument to assess awareness and perceptions of GenAI's failure modes and risks.
- Validation with subject matter experts and deployment on a sample of 960 U.S.-based participants.
- Structuring the instrument around scenarios grounded in publicly reported incidents and a taxonomy of GenAI's failure modes.

**Results:**
- Insights into public awareness and perceptions of GenAI risks and failure modes.
- Identification of gaps in understanding and areas for public education.
- Foundation for developing strategies to address GenAI risks and improve public trust.
