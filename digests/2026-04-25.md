### [From Research Question to Scientific Workflow: Leveraging Agentic AI for Science Automation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21910v1)
**Authors:** Bartosz Balis, Michal Orzechowski, Piotr Kica | **Published:** 2026-04-23

**Approach:**

**Results:**

- Highlights the need for further development and testing of such architectures in real-world scientific applications.

### [Alignment has a Fantasia Problem](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21827v1)
**Authors:** Nathanael Jo, Zoe De Simone, Mitchell Gordon | **Published:** 2026-04-23

**Core Problem:** Current AI systems treat user prompts as complete expressions of intent, which can lead to failures known as Fantasia interactions, where the system appears useful but is not aligned with the user's needs.

**Approach:**
- Identifies the limitations of assuming users can clearly articulate their goals and the kind of assistance they need.
- Proposes a rethinking of alignment research to provide cognitive support for users in forming and refining their intent over time.
- Advocates for an interdisciplinary approach combining machine learning and insights from behavioral research.

**Results:**
- Highlights the importance of recognizing and addressing Fantasia interactions for more effective and safe AI systems.
- Suggests that AI should actively help users refine their goals and understanding of what they need.
- Emphasizes the need for ongoing research into more interactive and supportive AI systems.

### [Transient Turn Injection: Exposing Stateless Multi-Turn Vulnerabilities in Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21860v1)

**Results:**
- Demonstrates the effectiveness of TTI in uncovering vulnerabilities in state-of-the-art LLMs from various providers.
