### CrossCommitVuln-Bench: A Dataset of Multi-Commit Python Vulnerabilities Invisible to Per-Commit Static Analysis
**Authors:** Arunabh Majumdar | **Published:** 2026-04-23
**Core Problem:** This paper addresses the gap in vulnerability detection where per-commit static analysis may not be sufficient to identify exploitable conditions introduced across multiple commits.
**Approach:**
- The authors curate a benchmark of 15 real-world Python vulnerabilities where the exploitable condition was introduced across multiple commits.
- They manually annotate each CVE with its contributing commit chain and structured rationale for why each commit evades per-commit analysis.
- Baseline evaluations are performed using Semgrep and Bandit in both per-commit and cumulative scanning modes.
**Results:**
- The per-commit detection rate (CCDR) is 13% across all 15 vulnerabilities, indicating that 87% of chains are invisible to per-commit SAST.
- Both per-commit detections are qualitatively poor, with one occurring on commits framed as security fixes and the other having limitations.
- The study highlights the need for cumulative scanning modes to improve vulnerability detection.

**Results:**
- The evaluation uncovers significant vulnerabilities in large language models, highlighting the need for improved moderation techniques.

### MathDuels: Evaluating LLMs as Problem Posers and Solvers
**Authors:** Zhiqiu Xu, Shibo Jin, Shreya Arya | **Published:** 2026-04-23
**Core Problem:** This paper addresses the limitation of existing evaluations that cast models solely as solvers of fixed problem sets.
**Approach:**
- The authors introduce MathDuels, a self-play benchmark in which models occupy dual roles: each authors math problems under adversarial prompting and solves problems authored by every other participant.
- Problems are produced through a three-stage generation pipeline and validated by an independent verifier.
- A Rasch model jointly estimates solver abilities and problem difficulties.
**Results:**
- The study provides a new perspective on evaluating large language models as both problem posers and solvers.
- MathDuels enables a more comprehensive assessment of model capabilities.
- The results highlight the importance of considering both problem-posing and problem-solving abilities in evaluations.
